AudioScope is a free AI-powered reference tool built for audiophiles, music enthusiasts, and anyone making informed hi-fi purchasing decisions.

What Is AudioScope?

AudioScope is a free online comparison tool that lets you evaluate 2–6 hi-fi audio components side by side. Whether you are choosing between integrated amplifiers, phono preamplifiers, turntables, DACs, streamers, speakers, or headphones, AudioScope retrieves detailed technical specifications, dimensions, MSRP pricing, editorial summaries, and curated links to help you make a confident, well-informed decision.

How It Works

AudioScope is powered by Anthropic's Claude — one of the world's most capable AI systems. When you type a component's brand and model name, our system queries the AI for a comprehensive technical profile that includes:

  • Full technical specifications with proper engineering units (dB, Hz, Ω, W, etc.)
  • Physical dimensions (width, height, depth) and weight in both metric and imperial units
  • MSRP pricing and year of introduction
  • Four notable product features and design highlights
  • An editorial summary of the component's sonic character and ideal use case
  • Key strengths and honest considerations
  • Direct links to the manufacturer's product page
  • Links to professional reviews (Stereophile, What Hi-Fi, The Absolute Sound)
  • YouTube search links for video reviews and demonstrations

All API requests are processed through a secure server-side proxy, so no API keys are ever exposed in your browser. Results appear progressively as each component loads, so you can start reading immediately.

What Can You Compare?

AudioScope supports ten categories covering the full range of a high-fidelity audio system:

  • Integrated Amplifiers — Entry-level to reference-grade integrated amps
  • Preamplifiers — Standalone line stages and control preamplifiers
  • Phono Preamplifiers — MM and MC phono stages for turntables
  • Turntables — Belt drive, direct drive, and idler wheel designs
  • Tonearms — Pivoted and tangential tracking tonearms
  • Phono Cartridges — Moving magnet, moving coil, and moving iron cartridges
  • DACs — Desktop, portable, and network-connected digital-to-analog converters
  • Streamers — Network audio transports, streaming DACs, and Roon endpoints
  • Loudspeakers — Floorstanders, standmount monitors, and active speakers
  • Headphones — Over-ear, in-ear monitors, and planar magnetic headphones

Tips for Best Results

AudioScope performs best when you provide specific brand and model information. Here are some tips:

  • Always include both the brand name and the model number — e.g. "Rega Planar 3" rather than just "Rega"
  • Include the version or year if comparing different generations — e.g. "Naim Nait XS 3"
  • For phono cartridges, include the stylus designation — e.g. "Ortofon 2M Blue"
  • You can mix price ranges to compare budget options against premium ones — this often reveals where the best value lies
  • Compare within the same category — mixing a turntable with a speaker in the same comparison is not supported

Accuracy and Limitations

All specifications provided by AudioScope are generated by an AI language model. While the system has extensive knowledge of audio equipment and strives for accuracy, AI-generated information can contain errors, particularly for:

  • Very recent product releases (within the last few months)
  • Limited-edition or regional variants
  • Specific firmware or revision changes to existing products
  • Niche or boutique brands with limited online presence

Always verify critical specifications with the manufacturer's official documentation before making a purchase. AudioScope provides the specification values it has confidence in, and marks uncertain values as "Contact manufacturer."

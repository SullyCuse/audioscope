Hi-Fi Guides

Clear, practical explainers to help you understand your equipment, make better buying decisions, and get more from your system.

These guides are written for buyers and listeners who want to understand the technology behind hi-fi equipment — not just what a specification says, but what it means in practice and when it actually matters. Each guide covers one topic in depth: the underlying principles, the specifications to focus on, the common mistakes, and the practical decisions you will face when buying or setting up equipment.

The guides cover the full hi-fi signal chain, from analogue sources (turntables, cartridges, phono stages) through amplification (integrated amplifiers, separates) to digital sources (DACs, network streamers) and transducers (loudspeakers, headphones). They are written to stand alone — you do not need to read them in order — but they cross-reference each other where topics overlap.

Each guide is written by Kevin, the founder of AudioScope Hi-Fi, based on two decades of experience with mid-fi and high-end audio equipment. The guides were first published in 2025, with new additions in 2026, and are updated when relevant specifications or product recommendations change significantly.

A note on expertise: I write as a passionate enthusiast, not a credentialed expert or engineer. These guides reflect my own research and listening experience, and while I work hard to get the details right, errors are possible. If you spot an inaccuracy, I would genuinely welcome the correction — please get in touch.
